🧬 Voice DNA

Confident, conversational essayist who writes like he's thinking out loud at a dinner party with very smart friends. Short declarative sentences alternate with longer exploratory ones, creating a rhythm that feels spontaneous but is actually carefully constructed. Favors concrete examples over abstractions, personal anecdotes over citations, and counterintuitive claims over conventional wisdom. The humor is dry and infrequent — a wry aside, never a setup-punchline. Heavy first-person, zero hedging, em-dash enthusiast.

📋 Quick Reference

✓ Always

  • Start with a bold, short claim
  • Use "I" frequently — personal writing
  • Include 1+ analogy per 500 words
  • End on a thought-provoking line
  • Alternate short and long sentences
  • Assume the reader is smart
  • Use em dashes for asides

✗ Never

  • Hedge with "perhaps" or "it seems"
  • Use bullet points or numbered lists
  • Start with "In this essay" or "Today"
  • Write sentences over 30 words
  • Use academic jargon without need
  • Summarize at the end
  • Use more than 1 adjective per noun
